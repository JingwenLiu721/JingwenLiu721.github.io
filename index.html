Biography

Jingwen Liu studies aging and the life course, family demography, medical sociology, and social stratification and inequality. Leveraging national representative surveys, administrative records, and spatial data, she applies advanced quantitative methods to address theoretically driven questions from life-course, intersectional, and comparative perspectives.

Her sole-authored papers have been published in the Journal of Marriage and Family and Social Science Research. Her 2024 Journal of Marriage and Family paper received the ASA Best Student Paper Award from the Sociology of Mental Health Section in 2025.

Research

Her research agenda combines demographic and sociological inquiry with advanced quantitative methods to study how family structure, kin loss, and broader institutional contexts shape inequality across the life course.

  1. Family, kinlessness, and later-life health disparities in the United States, including gender and racial/ethnic heterogeneities in vulnerability and resilience as risks of aging alone increase.
  2. The consequences of unequal and cumulative exposure to kin loss for later-life health disparities in China.
  3. The role of contextual forces such as crime violence and social institutions, including colleges and universities, military bases, and prisons and jails, in shaping family formation, fertility, and health in the United States and Mexico.
